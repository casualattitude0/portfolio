About Me

I'm Xue Aaron — though most people in the dev community know me as Casual Attitude. I'm a creative technologist working at the intersection of interactive media and AI. My path started at the NTUT Programming Club, where I led operations and partnered with Google to host a machine learning course — my first step toward applying intelligent systems to real products.

Over the past six years, I've worked across the entire game development cycle — from concept and planning to coding and release. I founded an independent studio and launched Soul of Butterflies – Incubation, handling story design, puzzle systems, and everything from crowdfunding to commercial release. I've applied the same self-directed drive to AI: Tarowl, an AI-powered app on GCP, and A Slime, a local agent with RAG, LangChain, tool use, and web + desktop clients.

Today at Buho Interactive, I lead development on commercial titles including Braverse (CookieRun IP with Devsisters) and Wacky Boat, and built CI/CD infrastructure that keeps teams shipping fast. Outside client work, I'm actively building agentic systems — document ingestion pipelines, multi-LLM routing, WebSocket chat, and calendar/search tools.

I'm an independent problem-solver at heart: I break ambiguous goals into testable pieces, ship a working prototype before asking for help, automate repeat work (CI/CD, agent pipelines), and iterate from feedback. My toolkit spans Unity and C# for games; Python, FastAPI, and LangChain for AI; Vue and TypeScript for web; and Flutter and GCP where the product needs them.

My Process

Concept

Decompose the problem; define constraints, user flow, and success criteria before coding.

Prototype

Build a minimal end-to-end slice alone — a playable level, working agent, or live API — to validate assumptions.

Collaborate

Bring in art, QA, or stakeholders once the core loop works; reduce wasted coordination.

Deploy

Automate builds, tests, and agent pipelines so shipping is repeatable, not heroic.

Reflect

Document what broke, what scaled, and what to reuse — for the next solo sprint or team handoff.
